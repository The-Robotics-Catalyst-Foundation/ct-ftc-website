# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Three audiences, all served by the same public site: students and parents exploring whether to get into FIRST Tech Challenge; existing team members and coaches who need event schedules, team directories, and logistics; and volunteers and sponsors looking to judge, mentor, or support the league. No single audience is primary — pages should serve whichever of the three lands on them without burying the others.

## Product Purpose

The CT FTC site is the public hub for Connecticut FIRST Tech Challenge: a robotics competition for students in grades 7–12. It exists to drive three outcomes at once — recruit volunteers, help families find and join a local team, and keep the wider community informed on events, team standings, and news. Success is a visitor leaving with a concrete next step (an event to attend, a team to contact, a volunteer role to sign up for), not just informed.

## Positioning

The CT-specific hub: the one trusted source for everything happening in Connecticut FTC, as opposed to FIRST's own genericized national resources. It ties together local events, the team directory (Robolyst), and volunteering in one place for the state.

## Brand Personality

Bold, energetic, playful. The site should feel like the excitement of a competition floor, not a corporate brochure — punchy type, confident color, tactile interaction. Warmth still matters (this is a volunteer-run community), but it's carried through tone and content, not through a softer visual register.

## Anti-references

No emoji used as icons (feature cards currently use 📅 📊 🤝 — replace with a real icon set when touched). The "liquid glass" / aurora-glass, softly-blurred aesthetic explored on the `liquid-glass-bento` branch (twice merged and reverted) is an explicit anti-reference for the public site: don't reintroduce glass panels, aurora gradients, or muted glass palettes here. That direction stays scoped to internal admin UI only, if anywhere.

## Design Principles

- **CT-specific trust**: always foreground Connecticut content (local events, local teams, local orgs) over generic national FIRST material.
- **Multi-audience clarity**: every page should serve students/parents, coaches/teams, and volunteers/sponsors without forcing one audience's journey to bury another's.
- **Community as proof**: let real teams, events, and people carry credibility rather than generic marketing claims.
- **Gracious professionalism in tone**: FIRST's own core value — compete intensely, treat everyone with respect — should show up in copy and interaction, not just program materials.
- **One clear next step per page**: always give a visitor an obvious action (find an event, join a team, volunteer) instead of passive reading.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific requirements called out beyond standard good practice: solid color contrast, working keyboard navigation, and respecting `prefers-reduced-motion`. No formal WCAG level commitment yet.
