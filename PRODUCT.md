# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Prospective and current FIRST Tech Challenge team members and their parents researching or already involved in a Connecticut team; volunteers (referees, judges, event-day support) looking to sign up for a competition; and sponsors or community partners evaluating whether to support CT FTC teams and events. All three groups use the public site depending on the page — there isn't a single dominant audience, so most pages need to serve whichever of the three lands on them.

## Product Purpose

The Connecticut FIRST Tech Challenge site is the public home base for the state's FTC community: event listings, team information, and a volunteer registration flow. It exists to convert visitors — whoever they are — into people who take action for the organization, and success is measured primarily by volunteer sign-ups, since the tournaments are entirely volunteer-run.

## Positioning

The single, up-to-date place for Connecticut FIRST Tech Challenge events and getting involved — team members, parents, volunteers, and sponsors all find their entry point here and can act immediately, rather than just browsing a calendar.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: Volunteer registration is primary; "Contact us" is the low-commitment fallback for visitors not ready to sign up.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Find your place in CT robotics" — CT FTC makes it easy to find your place in Connecticut robotics, whether that's volunteering, mentoring, or joining a team, with no experience required.
- Belief ladder: (1) This is a real, well-run organization. (2) Volunteering is low-barrier — no experience or long-term commitment needed. (3) My time will make a real difference for CT students.
- Proof on hand: none collected yet. Add testimonials, event photos, sponsor logos, or press to `.impeccable/assets/proof/` when available.

## Brand Personality

Warm and community-first — the site should read as run by real people in the CT robotics community, not a corporate franchise page — balanced with enough credibility and polish to reassure sponsors and schools.

## Anti-references

Should not look or feel like an overly "gamer"/esports site (aggressive dark mode, neon, tournament-brand intensity — too much for a family/school audience), nor like a dated, cluttered, text-heavy early-2010s nonprofit or club website.

## Design Principles

- Community over corporate: feel run by real CT robotics people, not a templated franchise page.
- One clear next action, always: every page should make volunteering the obvious primary path, with contact as the fallback, regardless of which of the three audiences lands there.
- Credible enough for sponsors, warm enough for parents: professional polish without losing approachability.
- Legit, easy, meaningful: actively lower the perceived barrier to volunteering at every touchpoint — it should never feel unproven, high-commitment, or like a closed circle.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA as the baseline for this public-facing site.
